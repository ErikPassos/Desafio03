History


The appearance of the museum is not only related to the Italian architect Lina Bo Bardi. In early 1959, the critic and director of the State Museum José Valadares encouraged the formation of a committee to deal with the creation of MAM-BA. The idea was born of a meeting between Valadares and Wilson Lins, Secretary of Education and Culture of the State. They were part of this group, which met weekly: Carlos Bastos, Clarival Valladares, Diogenes Rebouças, Godofredo Fo, Mário Cravo Júnior and Walter da Silveira. Lina was then invited to be the director of the institution. Solar do Unhão, 17th-century heritage property of Gabriel Soares that has housed MAM-BA since 1963, was restored in 1959 by Lina Bo Bardi. Created legally this year, the institution was inaugurated with two exhibitions in the foyer of the Castro Alves Theater, its temporary headquarters, on January 6, 1960, on the Feast of Kings.

For the occasion, a stone of ten tons was placed in front of the Castro Alves Theater, at the suggestion of Mario Cravo, and, at the entrance, a display of vegetables and minerals from the Commodities Exchange of Bahia. Upon entering, visitors were faced with curtains delimiting the rooms, which contained the 20 statuettes of As dancers, by Edgar Degas, and the 31 paintings of Antonio Bandeira.

The initial collection of the MAM-BA had reference works, such as Alfredo Volpi's Casas paintings, The bull, by Tarsila do Amaral and donated by Gileno Amado, Bird seller, by Candido Portinari and donated by Assis Chateaubriand, Landscape, of Rebolo Gonsales and provided by Associated Diaries, and the sculpture in plaster Self-portrait psychological, by Flávio de Carvalho. The works of the collection constitute an enormous diversity, following the principles of Lina Bo Bardi, with names like Calasans Neto, Chico Liberato, Di Cavalcanti, Genaro de Carvalho, Jenner Augusto, Rubem Valentim and Sante Scaldaferri.

Proposing to the Government a reform of the Solar do Unhão, Lina repaired the roofs, practically destroyed, the Chapel, used as a garage, and the Warehouses, which worked as a warehouse, besides building the famous staircase that is in Casarão, composed of similar fittings to the ox cart system. The architecture of space itself is used today to promote a relationship with the works presented, through projects and exhibitions specifics sites that stimulate the dialogue.



Localização

The Museum of Modern Art of Bahia (MAM-BA) is located in Solar do Unhão, a historical site of the 16th century, on the shores of Todos os Santos Bay in Salvador. Founded in the early 1960s, MAM-BA was initially located at the Castro Alves Theater, having moved to the present address in 1963. The MAM-BA is one of the 12 state museums that are linked to the Institute of Artistic Patrimony and Cultural (IPAC), an authority of the Secretary of Culture of the State of Bahia.

In terms of structure, the MAM-BA has a technical space with services of conservation, restoration and museology; eight exhibition rooms; a theater; a library; and an art workshop that offers open courses of painting, engraving techniques, ceramics, drawing, handmade paper and sculpture to the whole community.

In its dependencies you can find paintings, sculptures, photographs and drawings by artists such as Tarsila do Amaral, Portinari, Flávio de Carvalho, Di Cavalcanti, Rubem Valentim, Pancetti, Carybé, Mário Cravo and Sante Scaldaferri.

Jam no Mam


In addition to one of the most beautiful views of Salvador, anyone who passes through the Avenida Contorno on Saturday afternoons can see a great movement of people and cars heading to Solar do Unhão, a 16th century building that is the headquarters of the Museu de Modern Art of Bahia. The reason is JAM at MAM, a project that takes to the Museum's weekend a special soundtrack, based on the best style of jam sessions.

The project is attracting artists from all over the world who, passing through Bahia, find a democratic space to listen to Jazz with "Bahia's accent" and to practice the art of improvisation alongside the band Jeleia Solar and of professional musicians who are reference in the local market.

JAM in MAM is a project of Huol Creations, has artistic direction of the musician Ivan Huol and happens every Saturday at MAM - Museum of Modern Art of Bahia, from 18h. It is a continuation of the jam sessions that were carried out between 1993 and 2001 in the Museum of Modern Art of Bahia. Also under the coordination of the musician Ivan Huol, they were called Jazz MAM and they happened in front of the church of Solar of the Unhão.

Excellent Brazilian musicians have already performed at JAM, as well as artists from the United States, Canada, Argentina, Colombia, Italy, France, Belgium, Germany, Austria, Ireland, Chile, Cuba, Algeria, Japan and Denmark among many other countries - and, of course, the cream of the Bahian instrumental scene!
